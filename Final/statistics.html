Statistics

  1. About 1 in 8 U.S. women (about 12.4%) will develop invasive breast cancer over the course of her lifetime.
  2. In 2018, an estimated 266,120 new cases of invasive breast cancer are expected to be diagnosed in women in the U.S., along with 63,960 new cases of non-invasive (in situ) breast cancer.
  3. About 2,550 new cases of invasive breast cancer are expected to be diagnosed in men in 2018. A man’s lifetime risk of breast cancer is about 1 in 1,000.
  4. Breast cancer incidence rates in the U.S. began decreasing in the year 2000, after increasing for the previous two decades. They dropped by 7% from 2002 to 2003 alone.
  5. About 40,920 women in the U.S. are expected to die in 2018 from breast cancer, though death rates have been decreasing since 1989.
  6. Overall, African-American women are more likely to die of breast cancer.
  7. As of January 2018, there are more than 3.1 million women with a history of breast cancer in the U.S.
  8. A woman’s risk of breast cancer nearly doubles if she has a first-degree relative (mother, sister, daughter) who has been diagnosed with breast cancer
  9. For women in the U.S., breast cancer death rates are higher than those for any other cancer, besides lung cancer.
  10. The most significant risk factors for breast cancer are gender (being a woman) and age (growing older).
